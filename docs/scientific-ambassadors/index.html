SPOCTT Scientific Ambassador Program

The Scientific and Public Outreach of Cell Types and Taxonomies (SPOCTT) program at the Allen Institute invites selected scientists to serve as Scientific Ambassadors, helping expand scientific training and outreach around cell type taxonomies and related tools.

What Scientific Ambassadors Do

  • Present SPOCTT-related content in talks, workshops, or classrooms
  • Provide formal or informal training on relevant tools and resources
  • Engage in awareness-building conversations within the scientific community
  • Contribute to broader outreach efforts across neuroscience

What Participation Offers

  • Access to a curated scientific slide library for immediate use in talks and teaching
  • (Optional) access to SPOCTT team members to prepare content and/or practice your presentation
  • Opportunity to contribute to a coordinated scientific training and outreach effort across the Allen Institute
  • Eligibility for up to one SPOCTT-supported (paid) trip per year for outreach or training (for non-Allenites; Allenite funding considered on a case-by-case basis)

What We Ask

  • Participate in at least two outreach efforts per year
  • Submit a lightweight report summarizing each activity
  • Share slides or teaching materials when appropriate to support reuse

Contact

For questions or to discuss this program, or if you have suggestions for other folks to include in this program, please contact the SPOCTT team .
